# 01 — Vision & Pillars

## Working Summary

**Void Yield 2** is an optimistic hard-sci-fi, browser-based incremental logistics game about building a space industry network from a scrappy startup into a private space corporation.

Players survey asteroids, mine resources, ship goods through changing orbital conditions, grow colonies, and eventually build automated logistics networks and local shipbuilding capacity.

The game is AFK-friendly: actions take real time (minutes to hours), and production continues while away until blocked by storage, fuel, life support, workforce, or route constraints.

## Core Fantasy

The player should feel like they are operating a serious orbital command network:
- routes are alive,
- bottlenecks are visible,
- decisions are strategic but readable.

## Design Pillars

1. **Orbital Logistics Is the Signature System**
   - Routes, transfer windows, and moving orbital positions create meaningful planning.
2. **Colonies Are Power With Obligations**
   - Colonies unlock people capacity, but require life support and advanced materials.
3. **Incremental Growth With Real Bottlenecks**
   - Storage, shipping, fuel, labor, and throughput constrain growth in readable ways.
4. **Readable Hard Sci-Fi**
   - Scientific framing is grounded, but player decisions must always be clear in UI.
5. **Stable Economy, No Speculation**
   - Earth and major buyers are fixed-price anchors for planning reliability.
6. **AFK-Friendly, Not Punishing**
   - Waiting is allowed; recovery should be clear and achievable.

## Tone & Visual Direction

Target mood: **NASA-industrial orbital command**.

- Dark orbital map as emotional centerpiece.
- Practical management panels and rate-driven dashboards.
- A serious operational aesthetic with moments of awe from movement and scale.

## Stage 0 Vision Principle

Before architecture, validate visual and interaction direction with disposable mockups.

Primary question:
> What does this game feel like to look at and manage?
